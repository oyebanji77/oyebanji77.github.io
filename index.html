What is thought?

Thought is often used broadly, for example, thought can be seen as "Everything that goes through our heads". Thought is restricted, as it excludes the five senses. A key part of thought are the beliefs that rest upon evidence and testimony.

There are two degrees of beliefs: 1)Accepted with slight to no attempt to state the grounds that support such beliefs. 2)The basis of belief is deliberately sought.

I. Reflective thoughts are random coursing of things through the mind. It involves a sequence of ideas and a consequence. One thought directly leads to another, as it aims at certain, specifc beliefs.

II. It goes beyond observation. "I only thought of it" is a note of invention, such as imaginative stories. It is not reflective, as they do aim at knowledge.

III. Belief has two parts. First, the grounds have not been considered, and second, the grounds have been examined. It could be superstition, adequate, or not. There is an attainment of belief. Prejudgements that can involve tradition, instruction, and imitation.
